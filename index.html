Beyond Data Filtering: Selective GradienT Masking

A new method for removing dangerous capabilities from Large Language Models by "localizing" knowledge into specific parameters that can be deleted later.

The Challenge

Why isn't data filtering enough?

Removing harmful data before training seems obvious, but it has critical flaws.

label_off

Labeling Noise

Classifiers aren't perfect. Even 1% of mislabeled harmful data (false negatives) can be enough for an LLM to learn dangerous capabilities.
attach_money

Cost at Scale

Labeling trillions of tokens with high precision is prohibitively expensive and difficult to scale.
leak_add

Entanglement

Harmful concepts are often mixed with benign data. Removing everything reduces the model's general capabilities.

The "Absorption" Effect

One of the most surprising findings is that SGTM becomes more effective as models get larger.

Even if data isn't labeled as "forget", the model naturally routes it to the "forget" parameters because those parameters have become specialized for that type of content. This is called "Self-Reinforcing Knowledge Localization".

Result: Leakage (bad info entering safe weights) drops significantly from 8M to 64M parameter models.

Robustness to Adversarial Attacks

The Threat

Can the model "re-learn" the bad stuff?

Traditional unlearning methods (like RMU) are brittle. If an adversary fine-tunes the model on a small amount of harmful data, the suppressed knowledge often bounces back immediately.

SGTM removes the weights entirely, making it much harder to recover the knowledge.

Tokens needed to re-learn harm

Standard Unlearning (RMU) 13M Tokens
SGTM (Ours) 92M Tokens

SGTM is 7x more robust to re-learning.
